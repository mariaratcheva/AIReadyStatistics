Measurable output quality ()
Principle. The quality of what is ultimately presented to the user, not only what the NSO exposes, should be observable and measurable across Routes 2 to 5. The architecture should make it possible to assess whether the figure, its context, and its citation survive redistribution intact. Rationale. Authoritative data can be correctly exposed on the input side yet degraded on the output side: stripped of caveats, misattributed, or served stale by an intermediary. Treating output quality as a first-class, measurable property closes the loop between dissemination and the user-facing result, and gives the readiness assessment (Section 4) and benchmarking protocol (Section 5) a defined architectural anchor. Scope note. Measurability is strongest where the NSO controls the boundary (Routes 2 and 4, via logs and service instrumentation) and weaker but still accessible for Routes 3 and 5, where evaluation relies on sampling AI-system outputs against authoritative sources.
