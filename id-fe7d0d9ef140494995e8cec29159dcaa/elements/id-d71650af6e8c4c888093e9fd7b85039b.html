Defining AI readiness for dissemination AI readiness for dissemination is the capacity of a statistical dissemination system to make its outputs (data, metadata, statistical analysis and related content) discoverable, understandable, faithfully conveyed and attributable by AI systems. • Being discoverable: AI systems can identify and mobilise the relevant outputs when they are authoritative for a user’s question. • Being understandable: AI systems can relate the request to the right concept, indicator, source, population, time period or geographical level. • Being faithfully conveyed: AI systems can access and reproduce the information with the statistical context (metadata) needed for proper interpretation. • Being attributable: AI systems can identify the official source and distinguish it from derived or third-party interpretations. The goal is a dissemination offering that is not merely machine-accessible but AI-consumable in a consumer-agnostic way, regardless of whether the AI system is internal or external, already known or yet to emerge.
